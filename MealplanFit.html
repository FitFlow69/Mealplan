Healthy Meal Prep Ideas

Day 1

Breakfast: Berry Parfait

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • Assorted fresh mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • ¼ cup granola

Directions:

  1. In a glass or jar, spoon a layer of Greek yogurt.
  2. Top with a layer of mixed berries.
  3. Repeat the layers until the glass is filled.
  4. Sprinkle granola over the top.
  5. Serve immediately or refrigerate for later.

Lunch: Quinoa Salad Jars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • Mixed salad greens
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Cucumbers, diced
  • Bell peppers, diced
  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Feta cheese, crumbled
  • Olives, pitted and halved
  • Fresh herbs (such as parsley or basil), chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  2. In a jar, layer the cooked quinoa, mixed salad greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, red onion, feta cheese, olives, and fresh herbs.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the jar and seal it tightly.
  4. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
  5. Shake the jar before eating to distribute the dressing evenly.

Dinner: Sheet Pan Chicken and Veggies

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons dried Italian seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the chicken chunks, bell peppers, broccoli florets, and sliced carrots.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with Italian seasoning, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss well to coat.
  4. Spread the chicken and vegetables evenly on a sheet pan.
  5. Roast in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

Day 2

Breakfast: Overnight Oats

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • Fresh fruits and nuts for topping

Directions:

  1. In a jar or container, combine rolled oats, milk, Greek yogurt, chia seeds, and sweetener.
  2. Stir well to mix everything together.
  3. Cover the jar and refrigerate overnight or for at least 4 hours.
  4. In the morning, give the oats a good stir and add your favorite fruits and nuts as toppings.
  5. Enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • Grilled chicken breast, sliced
  • Whole wheat tortilla or wrap
  • Greek yogurt
  • Cucumber, sliced
  • Tomatoes, diced
  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Kalamata olives, sliced
  • Feta cheese, crumbled

Directions:

  1. Lay the tortilla or wrap flat and spread Greek yogurt over it.

Dinner: Sweet Potato Black Bean Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: avocado, cilantro, lime wedges

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  2. Add the sweet potato and red bell pepper, and cook for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Stir in the black beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  6. Serve the chili hot, garnished with avocado, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice if desired.

Day 3

Breakfast: Protein-Packed Breakfast Burrito

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 whole wheat tortillas
  • 1/4 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup diced bell peppers (any color)
  • 1/4 cup diced onions
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: salsa, avocado, Greek yogurt

Directions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk the eggs and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add the diced onions and bell peppers. Sauté until softened.
  3. Pour the whisked eggs into the skillet and scramble them with the vegetables until cooked through.
  4. Warm the tortillas in a separate pan or microwave.
  5. Divide the scrambled eggs, black beans, and shredded cheddar cheese between the tortillas.
  6. Roll up the tortillas tightly, folding in the sides as you go.
  7. Serve the breakfast burritos warm with optional toppings like salsa, avocado, and Greek yogurt.

Lunch: Colorful and Nourishing Chickpea Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (any color)
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, sliced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, olives, and parsley.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Adjust the seasoning if needed.
  5. Serve the chickpea salad chilled or at room temperature.

Dinner: Salmon and Veggie Foil Pack

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced (any color)
  • 1/4 red onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried dill
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Tear off two large pieces of aluminum foil and place one salmon fillet on each piece.
  3. In a bowl, combine the sliced zucchini, yellow squash, bell pepper, red onion, minced garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, dried dill, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat the vegetables.
  4. Divide the vegetable mixture evenly and place it around the salmon fillets on the foil.
  5. Fold the foil over the salmon and vegetables, sealing the edges tightly to create a packet.
  6. Place the foil packets on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes until the salmon is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.

Day 4

Breakfast: Wholesome Avocado Toast

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of whole grain bread, toasted
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: sliced cherry tomatoes, crumbled feta cheese, red pepper flakes

Directions:

  1. In a small bowl, mash the avocado with a fork until smooth.

  4. Top with your choice of optional toppings like sliced cherry tomatoes, crumbled feta cheese, or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes.
  5. Serve the wholesome avocado toast immediately.

Lunch: Vibrant and Filling Buddha Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli florets
  • 1 cup shredded red cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/4 cup sliced avocado
  • 2 tablespoons hummus
  • 2 tablespoons tahini dressing (mix 2 tablespoons tahini, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, 1 tablespoon water, salt, and pepper)
  • Optional toppings: sesame seeds, chopped fresh herbs

Directions:

  1. In a bowl or plate, arrange the cooked quinoa, roasted sweet potatoes, cooked chickpeas, steamed broccoli florets, shredded red cabbage, grated carrots, and sliced avocado.
  2. Drizzle the hummus and tahini dressing over the ingredients.
  3. Sprinkle with optional toppings like sesame seeds and chopped fresh herbs.
  4. Toss everything together just before eating to combine the flavors.
  5. Enjoy the vibrant and filling Buddha bowl.

Dinner: Flavorful One-Pot Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz (225g) whole wheat spaghetti or pasta of your choice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced (any color)
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for garnish (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion, and sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and slightly softened.
  3. Add the bell pepper, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes. Cook for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth, dried basil, dried oregano, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add the spaghetti or pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  6. Once the pasta is cooked, remove the pot from heat.
  7. Serve the flavorful one-pot pasta hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Day 5

Breakfast: Refreshing Green Smoothie Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe banana, frozen
  • 1 cup spinach or kale leaves
  • 1/2 cup frozen pineapple chunks
  • 1/2 cup frozen mango chunks
  • 1/2 cup almond milk (or any other milk of your choice)
  • Toppings: sliced fresh fruits, granola, chia seeds, shredded coconut

Directions:

  1. In a blender, combine the frozen banana, spinach or kale leaves, frozen pineapple chunks, frozen mango chunks, and almond milk.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy, adding more almond milk if needed to reach the desired consistency.
  3. Pour the green smoothie into a bowl.
  4. Top with your favorite toppings like sliced fresh fruits, granola, chia seeds, and shredded coconut.
  5. Enjoy the refreshing and nutritious green smoothie bowl.

Dinner: Nutritious and Aromatic Lentil Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dry lentils (any color), rinsed and drained
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 can (14 oz/400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz/400ml) coconut milk
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups chopped vegetables of your choice (e.g., carrots, bell peppers, cauliflower, zucchini)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Cooked rice or naan bread, for serving

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Stir in the curry powder, ground cumin, ground coriander, and turmeric powder. Cook for another minute to toast the spices.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes (with their juice), coconut milk, vegetable broth, and rinsed lentils to the pot. Stir to combine.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the lentils are tender.
  6. Add the chopped vegetables to the pot and cook for an additional 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are cooked to your liking.

Day 6

Breakfast: Spinach and Mushroom Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: grated Parmesan cheese, chopped fresh herbs

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until softened and fragrant.
  5. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown.
  6. Stir in the fresh spinach leaves and dried thyme. Cook until the spinach wilts.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet. Gently stir to distribute the ingredients evenly.
  8. Cook on the stovetop for a few minutes until the edges start to set.
  9. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for about 15-20 minutes or until the frittata is set and golden brown on top.
  10. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  11. Slice the frittata into wedges and serve warm with optional toppings like grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh herbs.

Lunch: Light and Crunchy Asian Slaw Salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups shredded cabbage (green or Napa cabbage)
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced bell peppers (any color)
  • 1/2 cup sliced cucumber
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped peanuts or almonds (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, shredded carrots, sliced bell peppers, sliced cucumber, chopped green onions, and chopped fresh cilantro.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the sesame oil, rice vinegar, soy sauce, honey or maple syrup, grated ginger, minced garlic, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad ingredients and toss to coat evenly.
  4. Sprinkle with chopped peanuts or almonds, if desired, for added crunch.
  5. Serve the light and crunchy Asian slaw salad chilled or at room temperature.

Dinner: Satisfying and Flavorful Mexican Quinoa Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (any color)
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (15 oz/425g) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime

Directions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onion is translucent and fragrant.
  3. Add the diced bell pepper and corn kernels to the skillet. Cook for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  4. Stir in the cooked quinoa, black beans, ground cumin, chili powder, paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all the flavors.
  5. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the ingredients are heated through and well-coated with the spices.
  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and squeeze the lime juice over the quinoa mixture. Stir to incorporate.
  7. Serve the Mexican quinoa in bowls, and top with your choice of optional toppings such as sliced avocado, chopped cilantro, diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, and sour cream.

Day 7

Breakfast: Fluffy Whole Wheat Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or vegetable oil

Directions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, and melted butter or vegetable oil.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of the pancake batter onto the skillet for each pancake.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake, then flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  8. Serve the fluffy whole wheat pancakes stacked high and top with your favorite toppings such as fresh berries, sliced bananas, or maple syrup.

Lunch: Mouthwatering Chicken Caesar Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper to season
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 head romaine lettuce, washed and chopped
  • 1/2 cup croutons
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Caesar dressing (store-bought or homemade)

Directions:

  1. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken breasts and cook for about 6-8 minutes per side until cooked through and no longer pink in the center.
  4. Remove the chicken from the skillet and let it rest for a few minutes. Then, slice it into thin strips.
  5. In a large salad bowl, combine the chopped romaine lettuce, croutons, and grated Parmesan cheese.
  6. Add the sliced chicken on top of the salad.
  7. Drizzle Caesar dressing over the salad according to your preference.
  8. Toss the salad gently to coat all the ingredients with the dressing.
  9. Serve the mouthwatering Chicken Caesar Salad as a satisfying and flavorful lunch option.

Dinner: Delightful and Protein-Rich Veggie Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup tofu or tempeh, cubed (optional for added protein)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger powder or freshly grated ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Directions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and slightly softened.
  3. Add the bell pepper, carrots, broccoli, snap peas, and mushrooms to the skillet. Stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  4. If using tofu or tempeh, push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and add the tofu or tempeh cubes to the other side. Cook for 2-3 minutes until lightly browned.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), sesame oil, ginger powder or freshly grated ginger, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper.
  6. Pour the sauce over the vegetables (and tofu or tempeh) in the skillet. Stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes until the sauce coats the vegetables and everything is heated through.
  7. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  8. Remove from heat and serve the delightful and protein-rich Veggie Stir-Fry over cooked rice or noodles.
  9. Enjoy the delicious and nutritious meal!
